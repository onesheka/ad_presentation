Sponsored Products — variant comparison

Seven layouts for the Sponsored Products module on Navi's home screen. Each variant trades off image impact, ad density, and pricing model differently. Pros and cons are split into advertiser and user impact.

Pros Cons ⭐ Recommended based on funnel data
Baseline Current

Navi Homescreen — today

1 hero ad in the first viewport · pagination dots only

The current Sponsored Products module shows one full-bleed hero ad. ~1.x ads receive a measurable impression on first paint, and only <20% of users swipe through to deeper inventory. The variants below explore ways to lift first-paint impressions without losing premium-tier brand impact.

Variant A

2.5-up rail

2 ads in first viewport · ~25 swipes to see all 50

Advertiser impact

Pros

  • Large images → strong brand impact per ad
  • 2 ads get measurable impression on first paint (vs 1.x today)
  • Full headline reads — good for copy-led advertisers

Cons

  • Ads 3–50 depend on swipe (<20% do); long tail rarely seen
  • Only 2 slots per page × 25 pages → most advertisers never get page-1 placement
User impact

Pros

  • Image-forward, snackable
  • Familiar carousel pattern; low learning curve

Cons

  • Variety is limited unless user swipes
  • Can feel repetitive over time
Variant B

Paged 2×2 grid

4 ads in first viewport · 13 pages for 50 ads

Advertiser impact

Pros

  • 4 simultaneous measurable impressions (~2× current)
  • All ads treated equally → simpler flat CPM pricing
  • Grid layout supports more page-1 advertisers

Cons

  • Smaller images → weaker brand impact
  • Pagination still gates ads 5+; swipe rate bottleneck persists
  • Title truncates ("Free gold max" vs full headline)
User impact

Pros

  • Easy 4-way compare; good for "pick one"
  • No ambiguous peek — clear boundaries

Cons

  • Doubles vertical space on the home screen
  • Reads like a catalog, less "sponsored discovery"
Variant C

Hero + thumb rail

1 hero + 4 brand tiles in viewport · scroll for more

Advertiser impact

Pros

  • Hero slot = 100% view-impression rate → premium inventory
  • 4 thumbs get free measurable impressions alongside hero
  • Strongest hero brand recall of any variant

Cons

  • Two-tier pricing → ad-sales complexity, advertiser disputes
  • Thumbs have no value-prop copy → CTR likely low
  • Only 1 advertiser wins hero → scarcity tension
User impact

Pros

  • Clear hierarchy: primary + alternates
  • Fast brand recognition scan

Cons

  • Thumbs give no info about the offer
  • Feels app-drawer-like, disconnected from the hero ad
Variant D

Stacked list

3 ads in first viewport · "See all 50" → dedicated page

Advertiser impact

Pros

  • Full headline readable → best copy-led conversion
  • 3 ads get full-width measurable impressions
  • "See all" page = high-intent destination, can host all 50

Cons

  • 72×72 image is tiny → hurts image-led advertisers (fashion, lifestyle)
  • No premium slot → loses hero-tier revenue
  • Reads like notification list → ad-blindness risk
User impact

Pros

  • Fastest scan, full text visible
  • "See all" gives dedicated space to browse offers

Cons

  • Low visual energy — feels utilitarian
  • Loses the "discovery" delight of a sponsored carousel
Variant E

Hero + mini banner rail

1 hero + 3 mini banners in viewport (≈5 ads) · scroll for more

Advertiser impact

Pros

  • 4–5 ads get measurable impressions on first paint (vs 1.x today)
  • Hero preserves premium view-impression slot
  • Mini banners carry image + brand + value prop → better CTR than C
  • Second-tier still monetizable (not just logos)

Cons

  • Two-tier pricing still adds ad-sales complexity (shared w/ C)
  • Mini banner titles truncate ("10% back" vs "10% cashback on Swiggy")
User impact

Pros

  • Image-forward throughout, not just the hero
  • Good variety without losing primary focus
  • Alternates feel "ad-like" (consistent with hero)

Cons

  • Higher density — can feel busy on smaller screens
  • Mini value-prop text is small (may need zoom)
Variant F

3.5-up rail

~3.5 ads in first viewport · ~14 swipes to see all 50

Advertiser impact

Pros

  • 3.5 ads on first paint (~75% more than Variant A)
  • All ads treated equally → flat CPM, simple pricing
  • Image-forward — strong brand impact per ad

Cons

  • Smaller cards = smaller images than A → less brand canvas
  • Title truncates ("Gold max" vs full headline)
  • Long-tail still depends on swipe (<20% of users)
User impact

Pros

  • More variety visible at a glance vs A
  • Peek hint clearly invites scrolling

Cons

  • Cards feel cramped — text and image both reduced
  • Less "snackable", more catalog-y at small scale
Variant G ⭐ Recommended

Hero + thumbs with title

1 hero + 3.5 brand thumbs in viewport (≈4–5 ads) · scroll for more

Best balance — premium hero + brand-recall thumbs with offer text upfront. Highest measurable-impression count with clearest visual hierarchy.

Advertiser impact

Pros

  • 4–5 ads get measurable impressions on first paint (vs 1.x today)
  • Hero preserves premium 100% view-impression slot
  • Brand-color thumbs aid recognition vs neutral chips
  • Offer text on each thumb drives CTR (vs C's logo-only)
  • Smallest module footprint — leaves room for other home content

Cons

  • Two-tier pricing → ad-sales complexity (shared with C, E)
  • Smaller image canvas — limits image-led advertisers
  • Offer copy must stay short (≤12 chars) to fit single line
User impact

Pros

  • Clearest hierarchy of any variant — hero is unambiguously primary
  • Offer text upfront answers "what's in it for me"
  • Compact — feels less ad-heavy on the home screen

Cons

  • Smaller targets to tap (~92×88px)
  • Brand-color cluster could read as a catalog row
